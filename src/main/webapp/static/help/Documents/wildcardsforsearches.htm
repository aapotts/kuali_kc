Wildcards for Searches

You may use wildcard (%) and truncation (*) symbols when you only know part of a search criteria:

Use the wildcard and truncation symbols to create searches where there are unknown characters, multiple spellings, or various beginnings/endings. 

•      Enter the prefix, suffix, or root of a search term and replace the missing portion with an asterisk to find all forms of that word.  For example, you might enter Smith* in a last name field to find all last names that begin with Smith, such as Smith, Smithson, Smithington, etc.

•      Use the wildcard (%) in your search criteria to replace each unknown character in a term.  This lets you find all occurrences of that word that have any letter in place of the percent symbol.  For example, you might enter x%y%z and find these words: a, b, c.
