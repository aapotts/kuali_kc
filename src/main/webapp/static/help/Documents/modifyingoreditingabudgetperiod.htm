Modifying or Editing a Budget Period

•      Make your changes to the Period Start Date and/or Period End Date.

•      Click  to make these changes on screen, but not save them in the latest saved version.

•      Click  to make these changes in the most recently saved version of the document.

You may make any number of modifications to the budget period start dates and end dates and keep them on–screen by clicking  and not clicking .

You must click  to make your changes permanent. 

If you attempt to go to another tab before saving new or modified budget periods, KC prompts you to confirm (yes) or cancel (no) your changes if they will cause recalculations of the budget. 

Unsaved changes remain visible until you click .  This forces KC to delete your changes and go back to the most recently saved version of your document.

Failure to either save or reload may result in error messages on other tabs.

 

When you create a new budget version, the start date of period 1 cannot be earlier than the Project Start Date, nor can the end date of the final period exceed the Project End Date. 

You may generate multiple budget periods of various lengths as long as they are sequential and fall between the Project Start Date and Project End Date.

You may enter the Total Requested Cost, Direct Cost, Indirect Cost, Unrecovered F&A, and Cost Sharing for each budget period, but this is not required.  (See Error! Reference source not found. below.)

After you enter all necessary Personnel and Non-Personnel expenses for Budget Period 1, you can load future periods by clicking .  KC automatically takes the Total Sponsor Cost, Direct Cost, Cost Sharing, Indirect Costs, Unrecovered F&A, and Cost Sharing figures from budget period 1 and rolls them into future budget periods.  KC also applies the inflation rate to the expenses entered for budget period 1 if you indicate it on the appropriate Line Item Details sub–panel on the Personnel and Non–Personnel tabs.

KC displays totals for the Total Sponsor Cost, Direct Cost, Unrecovered F&A, and Cost Sharing columns at the bottom of the panel.
